Attributes & Attitudes

What's the difference between conventional and organic anyway? Were avocado's truly that binary in nature? Well no, this actually refers to the farming method implemented to grow these fruits. Conventional farming uses chemical fertilizers to promote plant growth, while organic farming employs the traditional manure and compost to fertilize the soil. Sure conventional is faster, but organic is said to be healthier, with these avocado's containing more healtheir nutrients.

But in spite of these healthier benefits, at no point in America's years from 2015 to 2018 do organic avocado's outdo conventional avocado's in sales. In fact the sales of organic avocado's were near non-existent come the start of 2018.

Now when the avocado is sold, it's stuck with a particular kind of receipt. What's the most prevalent?


An Avocado Array

So when avocado's are sold, it is attached with one of the three receipts: PLU 4046, PLU 4225 and/or PLU 4770. The product/price lookup (shortened to PLU) code serves to uniquely identify a produce. And for avocado's, there are three in particular. 4046 identifies non-organic small/medium Avocados, 4225 identifies non-organic large Avocados, and 4770 identifies non-organic extra large Avocados.

Looking at the chart,, it's a bit of an even split between PLU 4046 and PLU 4225. Some regions showcase more purchasing of 4046, and others 4225. It is not clear that either is dominant. At the very least, we definitely know that no common American purchases extra large avocado's on the daily. Wait, affordability. Yes, that is where we'll stop at next.
